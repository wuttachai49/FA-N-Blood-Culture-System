🔬 Technical Specifications & Detection Methods

Detection Principle: Colorimetry Technology

The FA-N Series employs non-invasive colorimetry detection using advanced optical sensors and intelligent algorithms to monitor microbial growth in real-time.

How It Works:

  1. Microorganism Growth: Bacteria/fungi produce CO₂, changing pH in the culture medium
  2. Chemical Reaction: pH changes alter the chromogenic film color at the bottle bottom (dark → light)
  3. Light Transmission: More light passes through as the film color lightens
  4. Optical Detection: Photodetector measures reflected light intensity
  5. Algorithm Analysis: System algorithms analyze reflectivity curves to determine positive/negative status

Advanced Detection Algorithms

The FA-N Series employs multiple sophisticated algorithms tailored to different growth patterns, including early detection for organisms with delayed lag phases.

🚀 Early Algorithm (0.5 - 4 Hours)

Early Acceleration Method

Detection Time: 1h 20min

For organisms with short lag phases, detects slope acceleration trend in early culture stage.

⚡ Early Detection Methods

Early Rate Method

Detection Time: 1h

For delayed bottles (missed lag phase), detects slope change in initial culture stage.

📊 Threshold Method

Detection Time: 1h 40min

Detects absolute reflectance reaching critical threshold for delayed bottles.

📈 Normal Algorithm (2+ Hours)

Rate Method

Detection Time: 3h 40min

For bottles loaded on time, detects positive results by growth slope trend.

🔄 Slow Growth Algorithm

Specialized detection for slow-growing bacteria and weak-growing organisms with extended logarithmic phases.
