Growth Curve Analysis

Bacterial Growth Phases & Detection Mechanisms

📈 Bacterial Growth Curve Phases

The FA-N Series detects growth starting from the LOG PHASE (exponential growth), enabling early results in as little as 1 hour for rapid-growing organisms.

Growth Curve Overview

Phase 1: LAG (0-2 hours)

Bacteria adjust to environment. Minimal cell division. No CO₂ production. NOT detected

Phase 2: LOGARITHMIC (2-6 hours)

Rapid multiplication. CO₂ & acids produced. pH decreases. 1-4 hours

Phase 3: STATIONARY (6-7+ hours)

Growth = death rate. Population stabilizes. Confirmed positive

Phase 4: DEATH (7+ hours)

Cell death > reproduction. Nutrient depletion. Maintained positive

🔑 Key Insight: The FA-N Series can detect growth starting from LOG PHASE. Early detection algorithms capitalize on the rapid rate of change during exponential growth, enabling results 3-6 hours faster than waiting for stationary phase.
🔬 Detection Mechanism

Non-invasive colorimetry detects chromogenic film color changes caused by bacterial metabolic byproducts.

How It Works

Stage Process Result
